1. PROPERTY ISSUE SUMMARY

A detailed visual and infrared thermographic inspection was carried out on 27 September 2022 for a flat in an 11-floor residential building with no history of previous structural audits or repairs. The inspection identified 7 distinct impacted areas spanning the Hall, Bedroom, Master Bedroom, Kitchen, Common Bathroom, and Parking Area — all exhibiting active dampness, seepage, or water staining.

Thermal imaging using a Bosch GTC 400 C Professional camera confirmed moisture presence across all reported areas, with temperature differentials ranging from 3 °C to 8 °C between wet and dry surfaces — indicating ongoing, active moisture infiltration rather than historic or dried-out staining.

The primary root cause is failed waterproofing in wet areas (open tile joints, hollow tiles, and damaged Nahani traps in WC and bathrooms), which allows water to saturate floor slabs and migrate into adjacent rooms via capillary action. A compounding cause is cracks in external walls allowing rainwater ingress. Critically, a leak from Flat 203 above is also contributing to dampness in Flat 103's common bathroom ceiling — requiring multi-unit coordination. Leakage is confirmed as occurring all the time (not only during monsoon), indicating plumbing or waterproofing failure rather than a purely weather-driven issue.

⚠️ Immediate attention required for Parking Area seepage and Common Bathroom ceiling dampness — these involve active water passing through a structural floor slab, which risks long-term structural integrity if untreated.

5. RECOMMENDED ACTIONS

Priority 1 Immediate Actions — Complete Within 4 Weeks

  1. Fix Concealed Plumbing & Nahani Trap (WC / Common Bathroom)
    Engage a licensed plumber to inspect and repair all concealed plumbing lines in the WC area. Replace the damaged Nahani trap. Repair all loose plumbing joints (flush tank, angle cock, bibcock connections). Pressure-test all repaired lines before any waterproofing work begins. Do not start tile grouting until plumbing is confirmed leak-free.
  2. Bathroom & WC Tile Grouting Treatment (All Bathrooms in Flat 103)
    Clean all tile surfaces. Cut existing grout joints into a V-shape using an electric grout cutter. Fill joints with a liquid polymer-modified mortar (e.g. Dr. Fixit URP or equivalent) to ensure the material reaches cracks below tiles. After initial set, fill RTM grout into tile joints and patch. Apply polymer-modified mortar (PMM) at all outlets and wall-floor corners. Allow full air cure for 24–48 hours before allowing water contact.
  3. Coordinate with Flat 203 Residents / Society Committee
    Report findings to the building committee. Request Flat 203's bathrooms and drain outlets be inspected and repaired urgently — identical grouting treatment and outlet fixing required in Flat 203 to stop ceiling dampness in Flat 103's Common Bathroom.
  4. Seal External Wall Cracks Near Master Bedroom & Repair Duct Pipe Gaps
    Fill all external wall cracks with waterproof crack-filler. Seal gaps around all external duct pipe penetrations using non-shrink polymer grout or weatherproof sealant. Replace corroded sections of external duct pipes if identified during close inspection.

Priority 2 Secondary Actions — Complete Within 3 Months

  1. Plaster Repair (Internal Damp Areas)
    After waterproofing source is fixed and walls have dried (minimum 4–6 weeks), chip off all loose, damaged, and hollow plaster in affected rooms. Apply a bonding coat of polymer modified bonding agent (e.g. Dr. Fixit Pidicrete URP, 1:1 ratio with cement) before re-plastering. Apply 20–25 mm sand-faced cement plaster in two coats with a shrinkage-compensating waterproofing additive mixed into both coats.
  2. External Wall Waterproof Coating
    After cracks and plaster are repaired, apply a premium waterproof exterior coating on all external walls. Current paint type is unknown ("Not Sure" per inspection) — existing paint may not provide adequate waterproofing. Apply anti-algae/anti-fungal primer before topcoat to address observed algae and moss growth.
  3. External Plumbing Pipe Repair
    Replace or repair all external plumbing pipes showing cracks or leakage (confirmed as Moderate on inspection checklist). Ensure all pipe-to-wall interface points are properly grouted and sealed.

Priority 3 Preventive & Long-Term — 3–6 Months

  1. Re-paint interior walls of all previously damp rooms with anti-moisture interior paint once fully dry.
  2. Schedule a follow-up thermal inspection 6 months after all repairs to verify moisture has been eliminated.
  3. Arrange annual building façade inspection given the structure's age and lack of prior maintenance history.
  4. Consider a full building terrace/roof inspection — terrace was not included in this inspection scope but overhead leakage from Flat 203 suggests terrace drainage should also be checked.
